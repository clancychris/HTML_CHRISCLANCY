3 episodes where I have faced issues and overcome them.

  • When I first did a job as a music producer, I was working alongside my all-time favourite producer who has done a lot of my favourite records. I had never recorded a drum kit, put microphones on a drum kit, and this was my first job working with another artist. I spent the days leading up to this educating myself from anywhere I could on the best ways to tackle these holes in my knowledge. When I arrived at the studio, I set everything up and the producer I mentioned praised me on my knowledge of drum tuning, microphone placement and how confident I was at it.
  • In my last job role before I started my own business, I worked for an e-commerce company. They were 18 months behind in their listing across various platforms. Some items would be listed on eBay, some on Amazon, but everything was inconsistent. I spent a few day learning about csv files and bulk uploading/listing. Comparing data from eBay/Amazon I noticed there were a lot of similarities and it was mainly the column arrangement that differed. I spent a few weeks teaching myself how to write macros using Excel and a while later, I automated the entire process. New items could be listed on both platfroms using the macros I created, also listings could be ported from one platfrom to another. I also learned photoshop automation so the product photos would be automatically generated, put into folders with a naming scheme and uploaded along with the products. After 4 weeks I didn't have much of a job left to do, but I had caught up on 18 months of backlog.
  • In my current role there is a lot of manual work which is very time consuming. Doing an album, if there are 12 tracks and I change a sound on one of them, it needs updating on the other 11. This can be an hour long job. I have used tools such as applescript and soundflow to automate many of the processes. I can now hit one button, walk away from the computer and come back an hour later to find the job completed. This helps me focus on the more creative aspects of my work and not get bogged down with some of the more tiresome work. I have actually grown to enjoy the automation process/scripting more than the work I am doing which is why I am going down this path.
