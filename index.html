Bio:


I am a developer currently studying Python, HTML, CSS, Javascript, Node, React, MonogoDB, Web3 and DApps

I have spent most of my life as, first a musician, and then as a music producer at a professional level. I have achieved a great deal of success in this field including working with artists I listened to growing up and even being nominated for a JUNO Award (Canadian Grammy). I feel I've achieved everything I wanted to do in this field and it's time to move into something different. I have been teaching myself Python and learning more and more about roles and skills within the tech sector. I find coding fascinating and can't wait to learn more each day. I have always had an interest in tech and computer and I've been fortunate that my role as a producer enables me to work with computers and software on a daily basis.

Obstacles I have faced.

When I first did a job as a music producer, I was working alongside my all-time favourite producer who has done a lot of my favourite records. I had never recorded a drum kit, put microphones on a drum kit, and this was my first job working with another artist. I spent the days leading up to this educating myself from anywhere I could on the best ways to tackle these holes in my knowledge. When I arrived at the studio, I set everything up and the producer I mentioned praised me on my knowledge of drum tuning, microphone placement and how confident I was at it.
I used to work for an e-commerce company. They were 18 months behind in their listing across various platforms. I spent a few days comparing csv files and bulk uploading/listing. It took me a few few weeks teaching myself how to write macros using Excel, but I automated the entire process. New items could be listed on both platforms using the macros I created, and could be ported from one platfrom to another. I learned photoshop automation so the product photos would be automatically generated, put into folders with a naming scheme and uploaded along with the products.
In my current role there is a lot of manual work which is very time consuming. I have used tools such as applescript and soundflow to automate many of the processes. I can now hit one button, walk away from the computer and come back an hour later to find a job completed. This helps me focus on the more creative aspects of my work and not get bogged down with some of the more tiresome work. I have actually grown to enjoy the automation process/scripting more than the work I am doing which is why I am going down this path.
